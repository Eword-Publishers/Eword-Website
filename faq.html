Frequently Asked Questions

What is Eword Publishers?

Eword Publishers is a publishing firm based in Nairobi, Kenya, offering hybrid publishing services, preliminary services, and printing to authors worldwide. Our mission is to make professional publishing accessible.

What type of publishing do you offer?

We offer Hybrid Publishing. This combines elements of traditional and self-publishing, where authors pay for professional services but retain control over the publishing process.

Can you publish a book for a client and recover the cost from selling the book?

No, under our Hybrid Publishing model, the author is responsible for the production costs. We facilitate the production process for a fee.

Do you offer marketing services?

We do not offer direct marketing services. However, we provide guidance and consultancy on effective ways to get your books to market, including advice on social media promotion and book launching.

Can Eword Publishers help me write my book?

Yes, in addition to other preliminary services, we offer transcription (converting audio/video to text) and content development services to help shape your manuscript, even from just an idea or narration.

What are preliminary services? Are they necessary?

Preliminary services are steps like proofreading, editing, book layout, cover design, and ISBN installation that prepare your manuscript for printing and give it a professional look. While highly recommended to improve quality, they are optional if you provide a print-ready manuscript.

How much does it cost to publish my book?

The cost depends on the services required. Preliminary and online publishing services have fixed fees (see our Pricing page). Printing costs vary based on page count, quantity, size, color, and manuscript condition. Please contact us with your project details for an accurate quote.

What is your minimum order quantity (MOQ) for printing?

Our minimum order quantity for offset printing is 250 copies. Printing smaller quantities is less economical per unit.

What are your typical timelines?

If your manuscript is ready for printing, the process (printing and delivery) takes 14 days. If preliminary services are needed, add an additional 14 days for a standard book (up to 180 pages). Larger books may take longer.

How much is an ISBN?

We provide an ISBN for Ksh 1,500 for clients using our publishing services. Authors can also obtain an ISBN directly from the Kenya National Library Services (KNLS) if preferred.
